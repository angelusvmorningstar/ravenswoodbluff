Storytelling Advice

Running Blood on the Clocktower well is a craft. These articles are about learning it deliberately rather than by accident.

The Approach

Blood on the Clocktower is not a game where the Storyteller just reads from a sheet and waits for the players to sort themselves out. The Storyteller shapes the session: the pace of information, the timing of deaths, the bluffs given to the Demon, the moment the reveal lands. Done well, the players leave feeling like they just experienced something. Done poorly, they leave feeling like they were processing rules.

The articles here are long-form and specific. They are not tips lists. Each one takes a single aspect of Storytelling and goes deep on it, with examples from real play and a clear argument for why the approach matters.

Topics Covered

  • Timing and pacing – how to read the room, when to let a debate run long, and when to cut it short.
  • Bluffs for the Demon – the art of choosing which characters to hand out, and how to make them feel natural.
  • Information balance – how much to give the good team, when misleading information is fair, and when it breaks the game.
  • The Demon reveal – why the ending matters more than anything else, and how to make it land every time.
  • Mechanical edge cases – the interactions that trip up experienced Storytellers, explained clearly.
  • Running for new players – adapting your approach for groups who haven't played before without patronising them.
